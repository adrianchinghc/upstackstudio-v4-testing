## Design Context

### Users

**Primary decision-makers:** COOs, MDs, GMs at established Malaysian companies (RM20M–RM100M+ revenue) who have seen hundreds of agency websites and are skeptical of vendor promises. They arrive frustrated by failed software projects, broken operations, and the gap between their revenue growth and their operational infrastructure.

**Secondary scouts:** IT Managers, Operations Managers tasked with vendor research. They need technical credibility, process clarity, and ammunition to justify the recommendation internally.

**Context of use:** Desktop-first research during work hours. Often comparing 3–5 agency sites in tabs. Scanning quickly for signals of competence and fit. The decision-maker may spend 90 seconds before deciding to scroll or bounce.

**Job to be done:** Find a software engineering partner who can actually deliver — someone who won't disappear after kickoff, won't send juniors, and won't deliver something that breaks on their specific workflow.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Quietly excellent. Industrial confidence.

**Voice:** Direct, specific, confident. The voice of a firm that knows it's the best and doesn't need to prove it with superlatives. Short sentences. Hard stops. Numbers over adjectives. "We delivered 15× revenue growth for BookXcess" — not "We help companies grow."

**Emotional goals:**

- Immediate credibility (within 3 seconds)
- Quiet confidence (not arrogant, not desperate)
- Relief ("finally, someone who gets it")
- Trust through specificity (real numbers, real companies, real outcomes)

**Positioning:** AI-enabled software engineering. This is a core differentiator — not AI-replaced, but AI-accelerated. Surface throughout.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Refined industrial authority. Dark-first with a genuine light mode. Typographically dominant. The visual equivalent of a firm that has seen everything and is quietly the best at it.

**The memorable thing:** When someone sees this site, they should think: _"I didn't know a software agency website could look like this."_

**References (what to extract):**

- netguru.com — layout confidence, asymmetry, grid-breaking composition
- nimblestudio.com — typography dominance, tight tracking, type as primary element
- goodspeed.studio — animation sophistication, scroll reveals, page transitions
- gojilabs.com — overall quiet confidence, the sum of all parts

**Anti-references (what to avoid):**

- Generic SaaS landing pages
- Webflow templates
- Safe, predictable layouts
- Centered everything
- Stock photo aesthetic
- Rounded corners everywhere
- Gradient blobs as backgrounds
- Generic hero illustrations

**Theme:** Dark mode primary (`--bg: #000000`), light mode equally premium (`--bg-light: #FFFFFF`). Both must feel intentional — light mode is not an afterthought.

### Design Principles

1. **Bold over safe.** Every layout decision should pass the test: "Would this make a COO stop scrolling?" If it looks like a template, start over. Asymmetry, overlap, grid-breaking elements, unexpected spatial relationships.

2. **Typography is architecture.** Bricolage Grotesque for display (800 weight hero, 700 section titles). Inter for body. Type scale is non-negotiable. Type should do 80% of the visual work.

3. **Specificity builds trust.** Real numbers (15× revenue growth), real company names (BookXcess, Daikin, Teleme), real outcomes. Generic claims are banned. Every headline should fail if you removed the company name or number.

4. **One orchestrated moment > twenty scattered touches.** A single well-designed page load animation is worth more than micro-interactions on every button. Be intentional about where animation adds meaning.

5. **Both modes are premium.** Dark mode is primary, but light mode must be equally polished. Brand blue (#0A4DFF) as strong accent in both modes; lime (#E6FE52) for selective highlights. Test every section in both before marking complete.

## Accessibility Requirements

**Target:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance

**Mandatory:**

- Color contrast ratios meeting AA standards (4.5:1 normal text, 3:1 large text)
- Full keyboard navigation support
- Screen reader compatibility (semantic HTML, ARIA where needed)
- Focus states visible (using `--brand-blue` outline)
- Reduced motion support (`prefers-reduced-motion` media query)

**Motion policy:** Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. When enabled:

- Disable scroll-triggered animations
- Reduce or eliminate transitions
- Keep essential state changes instantaneous
