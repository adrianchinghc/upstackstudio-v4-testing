## 1. Linktree: A Billion-Dollar Page of Links

![linktree as an example of a simple app making millions of dollars](/images/blog/2025-03-linktree-as-an-example-of-a-simple-app-making-millions-of-dollars-1024x491.png)

If you know [**Linktree**](https://linktr.ee/), you knew it would be on this list.

From the day it launched in 2016, all it does is give you a page so you can link to other pages.

![linktree user links](/images/blog/2025-03-linktree-user-links-1024x440.png)

Just for that, Linktree now generates**$37 million annually** with over**50 million users** and a**$1.3 billion valuation**.

The idea took off when Instagram only allowed one bio link at the time, frustrating influencers, businesses, and creators.

![linktree link example](/images/blog/2025-03-linktree-link-example-1024x353.png)

But here’s what will _really_ make you mad: The co-founders built the first version in just six hours!

## 2. Bitly: The $100 Million URL Shortener

![bitly homepage to show example of simple app making a lot of money](/images/blog/2025-03-bitly-homepage-to-show-example-of-simple-app-making-a-lot-of-money-1024x422.png)

[**Bitly**](https://bitly.com/) is another well known app that proves you don’t need a complicated idea to make millions.

It**shortens URLs**—that’s its entire business model!

Why shorten a URL? Because long ones look ugly and scammy and get in the way of character limits on social media platforms.

Bitly wasn’t the first—**TinyURL** did it first in 2002. But Bitly came along in 2008, added better tracking analytics, and took over the market.

Plus, Bitly offers custom branded links for businesses, a feature they’re happy to charge for.

![bitly business links](/images/blog/2025-03-bitly-business-links-1024x397.png)

As of 2022, Bitly was pulling in**$100 million in revenue** with over**500,000 users**.

## 3. Calendly: $3 Billion Just for Scheduling Meetings

![calendly homepage](/images/blog/2025-03-calendly-homepage-1024x544.png)

[**Calendly**](https://calendly.com/)’s entire purpose is to lets people book meetings without back-and-forth emails.

Ok when we put it like that it actually sounds like it solves a huge pain in the butt.

Still, it competes against the likes of Google Calendar and Microsoft Outlook, both**free** alternatives, and yet boasts:

- $270 million in revenue (2023)
- A $3 billion valuation
- 20 million users, including 86% of Fortune 500 companies

At the end of the day, it’s a**calendar app–and it’s making billions.**

## 4. WhatsApp: The $19 Billion SMS Replacement

![whasapp homepage](/images/blog/2025-03-whasapp-homepahe-1024x482.png)

[**WhatsApp**](https://whatsapp.com/) is essentially**SMS but better**—before WhatsApp, SMS was expensive, and messaging apps like Facebook Messenger weren’t built for phone contacts.

But the capabilities were already there, it’s just that nobody thought to connect the dots.

Until, of course, WhatsApp bridged that gap in 2009 with a free internet-based messaging service resulted in:

- A Facebook acquisition of $19 billion in 2014
- 1.3 billion in revenue in 2024
- Just under 3 billion users worldwide

And best of all, WhatsApp’s revenue source isn’t consumers—it’s businesses paying for messaging features.

![whatsapp business](/images/blog/2025-03-whatapp-business.webp)

WhatsApp basically took a B2C industry and made it freemium with B2B as their targets.

Always nice when big companies subsidize stuff for us peasants!

## 5. Texts: The $50 Million All-in-One Messenger

![texts combining all messaging apps into one dsahboard](/images/blog/2025-03-texts-combining-all-messaging-apps-into-one-dsahboard-1024x470.png)

[**Texts**](https://texts.com/) was founded in 2020 and acquired in 2023 for**$50 million** by the owners of WordPress.

Its function? It aggregates messages from**WhatsApp, SMS, Telegram, Signal, Messenger, Instagram, LinkedIn, Discord, Slack, and more** into one inbox.

A simple but obvious solution—one that’s now worth millions.

We now want to hurry on over to the last example.

The one most guaranteed to send you into a city-destroying rage.

## 6. Pinboard: A 20K Bookmark Maker

![pinboard homepage](/images/blog/2025-03-pinboard-homepage-1024x459.png)

[**Pinboard**](https://pinboard.in/) isn’t the most lucrative app on this list, but it’s by FAR the simplest.

So simple that even if it made 20 bucks a month, we’d send an Obama not bad meme its way.

But it doesn't make 20 bucks a month–it makes**$20,000**!

Just to be a**digital bookmark** for people to save their favourite links!

You know, as in that feature that ALL internet browsers come with for FREE?

Yup that, Pinboard does that and it makes**$20K a month**.

Anyone could make a clone in an hour with a no-code app builder–in fact Pinboard’s founder clearly knows this—on his X profile, it literally says**don’t try to fight him**.

![pinboard x account](/images/blog/2025-03-pinboard-x-account.png)

To their credit,**Pinboard isn’t an overnight sensation or trend**. It’s been around**since 2009**.

**This** means from**2009 to 2025** people are still willing to pay**for a bookmarking tool**!
