## Limitations of Our Research

Our goal was to let you skip to the end of doing research so you go straight to the findings, and all our sources are from top-ranking results in Google search.

We think this is valuable, but defintiely not without shortcomings.

### 1. No Accounting for Developer Seniority

Our research does not differentiate between rates for senior, mid-level, and junior developers, which will vary significantly. 

This goes beyond the scope of this guide which is to offer a high-level comparison of rates across *countries*.

And ultimately, a more senior developer will always cost more than a junior developer in *any*country.

And a senior developer in Mongolia will always charge less per hour than their Australian counterpart.

### 2. We’re Uncertain Where the Numbers Come from

We don’t actually know the exact origin of the numbers provided by our 28 sources. 

The internet is a vast web of self-referencing and sources often cite each other without saying.

### 3. Many Countries Are Mentioned Just Once

A third limitation is that many countries were only mentioned in a single source. 

This reduces the accuracy and reliability of the rate estimates for those countries, as having multiple sources would help ensure the data is more consistent and trustworthy. 

However, we think it’s better to have a source than no source at all 🙂

Above all else, we at Upstack Studio think that the biggest limitation is using hourly rates for software development in the first place! 

## Hourly Rates for Software Development Isn’t Ideal

Quality coding requires flexibility as new issues and requirements get discovered and addressed. 

So software development doesn’t work with rigid timelines the way a construction project might.

![news article about construction project being delayed to compare it to software development projects](/images/blog/2025-04-news-article-about-construction-project-being-delayed-to-compare-it-to-software-development-projects.png)

But that ambiguity is why hourly billing can be risky if you’re the one paying.

Why? An hourly system **rewards the service provider for taking longer.** 

In the worst-case scenario, it opens the door to you getting misled or lied to.

And even in the best case, it’s still misaligned.

Believe it or not, your app idea probably isn’t *that*unique. 

If you’re building a “Netflix, but for insert here”, most experienced developers have built something like it before. 

And if we’ve built it before, we’ll know the basic requriements and how long those take to build.

It's just the customizations on top of that boilerplate that we need to figure out.

Which means we *should*be okay with project-based packages that give clients certainty on:

- Budget
- Development process
- Time to market

And you might wonder, well *what if the developer doesn't have experience building my type of software?*

To that we say, with *a world of developers to choose from*, why the heck would you hire one without experience?

## How to Hire the Right Offshore Developers

We’d like to address two stereotypes here:

The idea is if you’re based in North America, hire locally for quality or overseas for cost-savings, but never both.

We think this is absolute nonsense.

![indian developers as a joke about how commonly india isthought of as an offshore software developer site](/images/blog/2025-04-indian-developers-as-a-joke-about-how-commonly-india-isthought-of-as-an-offshore-software-developer-site.jpg)

There are competent and incompetent developers in *every*country.

Offshoring software development **lets you buy the same level of competence for less** on the other side of the world.

But [**once you compromise on that competency for cost-savings**](https://upstackstudio.com/blog/offshore-software-development-fail/), you’re not outsourcing.

So even if you don’t mind an hourly rate system, make sure you vet developers thoroughly by:

- Reviewing their portfolios and customer feedback
- Requesting a free strategy session or roadmapping workshop
- Ensuring at least 1-hour daily overlap for real-time communication
- Using tools to coordinate meetings, manage projects, and share mockups
- Making sure you and they share a common language

We cover all this and more in our** **[**guide to managing offshore development teams**](https://upstackstudio.com/blog/managing-offshore-team/).

Also, check out our guide to**[offshore software developer rates in the US](https://upstackstudio.com/blog/us-offshore-software-development/)** for a closer look at one of the most popualr yet cost-prohibitive spots in the world.  

But in summary, the idea is that you don’t want to mange them, *you want them to manage themselves*.
