## Yes, App Scalability Matters (Just Not Always)

Hang around developers and founders long enough and you’ll hear them *obsess*over scaling their apps and software products.

![picture of fishmonger scaling fish as a joke about scaling applications](/images/blog/2025-03-picture-of-fishmonger-scaling-fish-as-a-joke-about-scaling-applications-1024x683.png)

If you're new to app development, you might assume:

Truth is scalability is not a buzzword and while it’s *related*to growth, that’s oversimplifying it.

And the *real*truth is that [**if you’re new to app development**](https://upstackstudio.com/blog/how-to-develop-an-app-with-no-experience/),_scaling probably doesn’t matter yet_.

But when the time comes, knowing how it works can save your business, so fine, let’s cut through the jargon and break down what app scalability is all about.

## Understanding App Scalability

Scalability is just a fancy way of asking: _Can your app handle more users and heavier usage without crashing?_

![crowded train coach as an analogy about an app scaling to handle more users and usage](/images/blog/2025-03-crowded-train-coach-as-an-analogy-about-an-app-scaling-to-handle-more-users-and-usage.webp)

It’s not just more users–it’s also about managing bigger data loads (think Google Photos storing billions of images) and heavier computational tasks (like AI tagging those images without lag).

A truly scalable system keeps things smooth no matter how much the workload grows.

### Scaling Can Mean a Total Overhaul

Imagine starting a small bakery in your home kitchen, baking a dozen cookies a day using the oven you already have.

A month later, demand doubles, but the oven can handle it–just needs an extra baking sheet.

But say your cookies go viral and orders jump to 1,000 a day?

No amount of extra baking sheets will help–you need a new setup capable of mass production.

Apps are the same–a system that works for 10 users *could*be tweaked to handle 20.

But beyond a certain point, adjustments won’t cut it—you need new infrastructure to handle that new level of demand–and that’s where scaling comes in.

## 2 Main Scaling Strategies

There are two main ways to scale an app:

- **Scaling Up (Vertical Scaling)** – Throwing more power (CPU, RAM, storage) at your existing servers, though there’s a ceiling to how much you can beef up one machine.

-**Scaling Out (Horizontal Scaling)** – Adding more servers to share the load, which has the added benefit of preventing a single server failure from crashing the app.

Of course, the most serious of developers simply download more RAM.

![download more ram homepage as a joke about how to scale an app vertically by upgrading processing power](/images/blog/2025-03-download-more-ram-homepage-as-a-joke-about-how-to-scale-an-app-vertically-by-upgrading-processing-power-1024x475.png)

Kidding, most serious apps use horizontal scaling because it's a more future-proof method and spreads risk.

## 4 Key Considerations for Scalability

### Architecture Makes or Breaks Scalability

Netflix and Twitch don’t scale because of their tech stack—they scale because of**smart architecture**: CDNs, distributed servers, and load balancing.

Your system’s**design**, not its programming language or framework, determines its growth limit.

Sometimes, an app simply _can’t_ scale because developers built it without the ability for future scaling. It was meant to be a home kitchen, and no upgrade can turn it into a commercial bakery.

### Find and Remove Bottlenecks

Scaling efficiently is about **finding what’s actually slowing your app down and removing those limiting factors.**

Some usual suspects:

- Slow database queries
- User management at scale 
- Inefficient application logic

Different bottlenecks need different fixes. Throwing more servers at the problem might work—for a while—but it’s just an expensive band-aid that hides deeper issues.

### Create a Safety Net Via Redundancy

Scalability isn’t just about**speed**—it’s about**surviving failures.**

A single server going down shouldn’t take your whole app with it.

That’s why**horizontal scaling generally wins**—it keeps your app running smoothly, even if one part crashes.

### Minimize Additional App Maintenance Costs

When a decision is made to scale up or out, you can expect more:

- Servers
- Code
- [**Testing**](https://upstackstudio.com/blog/mobile-app-testing/)
- [**Debugging**](https://upstackstudio.com/blog/debug-mobile-apps/)
- Users complaining
- Things breaking down just as you fall asleep

All this guarantees higher [**monthly app maintenance costs**](https://upstackstudio.com/blog/monthly-app-maintenance-cost/), the only question is by how much!

This is why it’s not a good idea to just add more servers to handle more usage–responsible developers will identify bottlenecks and find the most cost-efficient way to scale.

Scaling is great—**but only if you can afford to maintain what you’ve built.**

## The Myth of The “Most Scalable Tech Stack”

As this guide is for non-technical readers, we won’t drown you in jargon–just know there is no single [**tech stack**](https://upstackstudio.com/blog/technology-stack/) that is inherently the “most scalable.”

Every time, it’s a result of how well a stack–from databases to backend frameworks–is architected to fit the demands and requirements of a specific app.

![oj simpson glvoes as a joke about tech stack being chosen to fit app requirements](/images/blog/2025-03-oj-simpson-glvoes-as-a-joke-about-tech-stack-being-chosen-to-fit-app-requirements.jpg)

A tech stack that scales well for a social media platform may struggle when applied to a real-time analytics system.

The key is designing one that aligns with your use case, traffic patterns, and growth trajectory.

## Focus on Building an Amazing App First!

Don't stress about scaling before you need to–if your home kitchen works fine, why stress about building a commercial factory right now?

Your real challenge as a non-technical founder is\*\* **[**building an app people will actually love\*\*](https://upstackstudio.com/blog/how-to-come-up-with-a-software-idea/).

If you reach a point where scaling becomes a real issue, congratulations—that's a great problem to have!

Until then, keep things simple and build for today.
