## Key North American 5G Statistics

Here are some key statistics on the state of 5G in the US as of November 2024 according to the [**Ericsson Mobility Report**](https://www.ericsson.com/4adb7e/assets/local/reports-papers/mobility-report/documents/2024/ericsson-mobility-report-november-2024.pdf):

-**Over 300 million people live in areas served by 5G low-band** while**210-300 million** people are covered by**5G mid-band**. 

-**53%** of US smartphone subscriptions are on**5G** networks.

- By**Q1 2024**, FWA had reached**9 million** connected business and residential locations across the US, offering**high-speed, reliable connectivity** as an alternative to wired broadband. 5G in the mid-band spectrum is closing the performance gap compared to traditional fixed broadband.
- The rise of 5G has led to an increase in**data plan diversity**, including micro-segmentation, service bundling, and commercial bundling.
- The US leads use of**5G in sports venues**, with**243 venues** having >**20,000 seats**, 

### 5G in The US VS Canada

According to [**Ericsson’s North American 5G report**](https://www.ericsson.com/en/reports-and-papers/mobility-report/closer-look/north-america), while both the US and Canada have made strides in 5G deployment, the US has a more advanced 5G infrastructure with:

- More coverage across low, mid, and high-band frequencies
- FWA leadership
- 150 MHz of unlicensed CBRS spectrum compared to Canada’s 80 MHz

On a global scale, both countries are still leaders in 5G adoption, so Canada-based businesses benefit just as much from incorporating the strengths of 5G into their mobile app development strategies.

## What Is 5G?

5G is the fifth generation of mobile network technology which offers faster internet speeds and more reliable connections – for context, Vodafone states\*\* **[**5G is up to 10 times faster than 4G\*\*](https://www.vodafone.com/about-vodafone/what-we-do/mobile-and-fixed-networks/5g) with peak speeds over 1Gbps

![](/images/blog/2024-12-5g-ccopatible-smartphone-showing-extremely-fast-internet-bandwith-1024x683.jpg)

For end users, this means faster downloads, less delay, and better performance.
